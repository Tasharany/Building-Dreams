PC Builder Guide

Step 1: Choose Your Platform

Decide whether you want to go with Intel or AMD. Each has its own set of compatible motherboards and CPUs.

Step 2: Choose Your CPU

Select a CPU that fits your performance requirements and budget. Remember, the CPU's socket must match your motherboard.

Step 3: Pick a Motherboard

Make sure your motherboard is compatible with your CPU choice. Consider the size of your case, the number of RAM slots, and the features you need.

Step 4: Choose Your Memory (RAM)

Decide on the size and speed of the RAM. Ensure it's compatible with your motherboard's specifications.

Step 5: Select Storage Options

Choose between HDDs for high capacity or SSDs for faster speed. You might want a combination of both.

Step 6: Choose Your GPU

Pick a graphics card that meets your gaming or professional workload demands.

Step 7: Select a Power Supply

Ensure the power supply can handle the total wattage of your components. Look for high-efficiency ratings and reliable brands.

Step 8: Choose a Case

Select a case that fits your motherboard size and has good airflow. Consider the number of drive bays and the aesthetics you want.

Step 9: Cooling System

Decide on air cooling or liquid cooling. Consider the TDP of your CPU and the size of your case.

Step 10: Assemble Your PC

Follow a detailed guide or video to assemble your components. Take precautions against static and organize your cables for airflow and aesthetics.

Step 11: Install the Operating System

Once assembled, install your chosen operating system via USB or disk, and then download the necessary drivers.

Step 12: Final Checks and Tweaks

Enter BIOS to ensure all components are detected. Configure your settings, and consider overclocking if desired and supported.
