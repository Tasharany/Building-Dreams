How to Build Your PC

A comprehensive step-by-step guide to assembling your PC.

Step 1: Preparing the Case

Remove the side panels of your case for easy access. Install the power supply and ensure the fan faces outside if it's bottom-mounted.

Step 2: Installing the Motherboard

First, place the I/O shield in the case. Then, align the motherboard with the standoffs and secure it with screws.

Step 3: Installing the CPU

Open the CPU socket, carefully place the CPU in without force, and secure the latch or retention arm.

Step 4: Installing Memory (RAM)

Open the tabs on the RAM slots, align the notches on the RAM sticks with the slots, and press down firmly until the tabs snap back into place.

Step 5: Installing Storage

Secure your SSD or HDD in the drive bays or mounting spots. Connect SATA data and power cables.

Step 6: Installing the GPU

Remove the necessary backplates from the case, align the GPU with the PCIe slot, and press it in until the latch clicks. Screw it into the case for support.

Step 7: Power Connections

Connect the power supply cables to the motherboard, including the main 24-pin connector and additional 4/8-pin CPU power connectors. Also, connect the GPU power cables if needed.

Step 8: Final Checks and Testing

Ensure all components are securely in place and cables are managed properly. Connect your monitor and peripherals, then power on the system to test. Enter BIOS to verify hardware detection.
